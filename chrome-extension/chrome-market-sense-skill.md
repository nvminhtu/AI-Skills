## 1. The "Money Pain" Signals (Must Have ALL 3)

### ‚úÖ 1. Willingness to Pay
The problem must already have people paying to solve it, or losing money because of it.
- **Good:** "My sales team spends 3 hours a day copying data to CRM." (Time = Money).
- **Bad:** "I want to change the background color of Facebook." (Fun = $0).

### ‚úÖ 2. Daily Repetition
The user feels this pain **every single day**.
- **Good:** Automating a task done 10x/day.
- **Bad:** A tool used once a year (e.g., "Tax calculator for 2023").

### ‚úÖ 3. The "Ugly" Competition
Are there existing solutions?
- **No competitors:** DANGER. Usually means no market.
- **Google/Facebook:** DANGER. Do not fight giants.
- **Ugly/Old competitors:** **GOLD MINE.** If a 2-star extension hasn't been updated in 3 years but has 10k users, **build that immediately**.

## 2. Review Mining: Finding the "Gap"
*Don't invent features. Read 1-star reviews.*

**Process:**
1.  Find the top 3 competitors in the niche.
2.  Filter reviews by "1 Star" and "Recent".
3.  Look for phrases:
    - "It works but..."
    - "I wish it had..."
    - "Too expensive for just..."
    - "It crashed when I tried to [Action]..."

**The Gap:** Build exactly what they complained about. That is your USP (Unique Selling Point).

## 3. The "Kill List" (Immediate Rejection)
